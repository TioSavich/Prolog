How Children Learn Arithmetic

A computational model of crisis-driven strategy development

A child starts with nothing but tally marks. One, two, three... it works. Until it doesn't. When counting gets too slow, something has to change. This site shows you what happens at that moment — and how every arithmetic strategy a child learns emerges from that kind of crisis.

You'll feel it yourself. You'll drag springs, watch machines snap, and see strategies build on top of each other like Russian dolls. No math background required — just curiosity about how learning actually works.

This project models children's arithmetic strategy development as a computational system where strategies emerge through perturbation and reorganization. Drawing on CGI (Carpenter, Fennema, Franke) and Steffe's constructivism, it formalizes the developmental sequence from counting-all through derived facts as a hierarchy of finite-state automata that invoke each other as subroutines.

Each strategy is a machine. When a machine hits its limits, crisis triggers the construction of a new machine that wraps the old one. The interactive tools let you see and feel this process — and evaluate whether the formalization captures what the research describes.

Carpenter, T. P., Fennema, E., Franke, M. L., Levi, L., & Empson, S. B. (1999). Children's Mathematics: Cognitively Guided Instruction. Heinemann. The CGI framework documents how children's informal strategies for arithmetic develop into increasingly efficient algorithms through teacher-guided instruction.
Steffe, L. P., & Olive, J. (2010). Children's Fractional Knowledge. Springer. Steffe's constructivism treats mathematical knowledge as constructed through the child's own operations, not transmitted from teacher to student.

A Prolog formalization modeling arithmetic strategy acquisition through crisis. The meta-interpreter (solve/6) models computing; the sequent calculus (proves/4) models justifying. Where formal proof goes hollow — at identity with Trace, S-O Inversion, double negation elimination — the system returns erasure instead of proof. Those erasure points mark where human judgment must enter.

The interactive tools enact the same structures the Prolog formalizes: Zeeman catastrophe machines for the snap, nested automata for strategy composition, spring-coupled physics for tension propagation. The formalization is a tool for noticing where formalization stops.

Brandom, R. (1994). Making It Explicit. Harvard University Press. The inferentialist framework: meaning is constituted by inferential role, not reference. A concept's content is its position in the space of reasons.
