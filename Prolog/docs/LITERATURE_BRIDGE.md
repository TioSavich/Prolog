## Strongest Bridge: Fractal Architecture

The project's core claim — every strategy has an Iterative Core wrapped in a Strategic Shell — maps directly onto:
- Steffe's number sequence hierarchy (each constructed by taking the previous as material)
- Gray & Tall's encapsulation hierarchy (counting → addition → multiplication)
- Pirie & Kieren's recursive embedding
- The RBC Model (Dreyfus/Hershkowitz/Schwarz): "building-with actions nested in constructing actions"

The project goes further by providing a *formal computational model* of this self-similarity.

## Deepest Connection: Productive Failure of Formalization

The literature is already documenting limits of its own explanatory frameworks:
- Steffe: metamorphic accommodation "cannot be identified in any moment"
- Hackenberg: learning requires conditions the teacher cannot fully control
- Simon: perturbation alone is insufficient
- Cobb: reflective abstraction cannot be expressed in computer formalisms

The project provides a *formal instrument for locating those limits precisely*. The arche-trace erasure boundary is the computational equivalent.

## Key Challenges from the Field

1. **Simon's critique**: Perturbation is insufficient. The ORR cycle must account for activity-effect reflection, not just resource exhaustion.
2. **Simultaneous vs. sequential**: Three-level unit coordination (MC3) requires simultaneous holding of all three levels. Sequential FSM subroutine invocation may not capture this.
3. **Reorganization, not wrapping**: Each new number sequence reorganizes the previous one (Steffe). The synthesis engine currently wraps, not reorganizes.
4. **Affective dimension**: Hackenberg's "subjective vitality" and "mathematical caring relations" — the energetic/emotional response to perturbation is part of the mechanism.
5. **Individual variation**: The field will ask whether automata respect that each child constructs unique trajectories.
