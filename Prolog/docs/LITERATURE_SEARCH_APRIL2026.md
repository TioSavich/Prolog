Venema, Y. (2025). Modal automata: Analysing modal fixpoint logics, one step at a time. In *33rd EACSL Annual Conference on Computer Science Logic (CSL 2025)* (LIPIcs, Vol. 326, pp. 5:1–5:5). Schloss Dagstuhl. https://doi.org/10.4230/LIPIcs.CSL.2025.5

## 4. Catastrophe Theory in Cognitive Science / Learning

## Strategic Convergences

1. **Brandom + discourse coding [§2, §5]:** Inferentialist semantics grounds what discourse analysts try to capture. Deontic scorekeeping is compatible with coding scheme design. PML could be positioned as the first discourse coding framework with explicit inferentialist foundations.

2. **Catastrophe theory + decentering [§4, §3]:** Teacher decentering may involve a bifurcation. Catastrophe criteria (bimodality, hysteresis, sudden jumps) could operationalize the moment of decentering transition. The More Machine isn't just a metaphor — it could be a measurement model.

3. **Modal logic + strategy automata [§1]:** The FSM strategy automata and PML operators could be formally connected through the automata-theoretic tradition in modal logic. This would ground the claim that PML characterizes classes of reasoning strategies.

4. **Epistemic stance + PML [§6]:** PML's S/O/N modes are epistemic stance distinctions. Boyd (2015) argues stance matters more than form — PML makes this operationalizable.
