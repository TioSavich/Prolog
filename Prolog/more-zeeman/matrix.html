Cantor, G. (1891). Ueber eine elementare Frage der Mannigfaltigkeitslehre. Jahresbericht der Deutschen Mathematiker-Vereinigung, 1, 75-78. The diagonal argument proves that the reals are uncountable by constructing a number that differs from every listed number in at least one digit.

Known limitations

Three things this system cannot do:

  • The synthesis engine wraps oracle calls rather than constructing genuine FSMs. A learner who "synthesizes" a strategy is memorizing a phone number, not discovering a method.
  • Fractions require three-level unit coordination. The current system handles two levels. This is a "not yet" — but the gap is real.
  • Brandom's third phase — recollection, the retrospective rational reconstruction that exhibits the course of experience as expressively progressive — has no computational model. The matrix records. It does not recollect.
