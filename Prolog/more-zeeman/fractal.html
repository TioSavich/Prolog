The fractal nesting visible here is the geometric analogue — each outer ring is a higher plane that presupposes the inner rings. COBO/RMB are context-free (they invoke subroutines). The fraction crisis occurs at the context-sensitive boundary, where flat FSM nesting breaks.

Negarestani, R. (2018). Intelligence and Spirit. Urbanomic/Sequence Press. Following Brandom's "laughing Santa" example, Negarestani describes finite-state machines as "automatic laughing bags" that recognize and produce regular languages. But "higher levels of syntactic and semantic complexity require more powerful abstract automata (context-free, context-sensitive, recursively enumerable)." The fractal rings show this hierarchy spatially: each outer ring requires stack depth the inner ring does not. What the visualization cannot show is the boundary where nesting itself breaks — where the system would need to modify its own transition table. That is the context-sensitive boundary, and it is where fractions live.
