what hermes does

Not a tutor. Not a grader. A listener.

Hermes joins your zoom call, your classroom, your one-on-one. It hears the math; it watches the talk; it has read every paper on cognitively-guided instruction. It has opinions, but it waits to be asked.

core01

⌒ Listens

Live transcription mapped, in real time, to PML codes and TalkMoves. You see exactly which utterance landed where.

5 practices02

~ Anticipates

Before the lesson: ranks likely strategies for each task. During the lesson: marks which ones actually showed up.

5 practices03

⌢ Pairs

Suggests which two students to put in a breakout — based on which divergence will be most productive to surface.

visualizes05

↗ Shows the math

tooling06

Hermes won't tell you the answer. He'll ask: what would you like the student to notice? — and route the conversation from there.

why "hermeneutic"

A calculator that interprets, instead of replacing.

A normal calculator gives you the answer. Hermes gives you the question — and the strategies behind it — and the misconceptions that compete with it. Computation is downstream. Interpretation is the product.

a normal calc

Returns a value.

You type 38+27. It says 65. The arithmetic is finished, the conversation never started.

a tutor bot

Returns a hint.

It tells the student what to do next. The teacher's expertise becomes ornamental.

hermes

Returns a map.

The strategies that might surface. The misconceptions that might compete. The pairing that might productively expose the divergence. The teacher decides; Hermes prepares the ground.
